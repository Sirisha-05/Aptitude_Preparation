Percentage — Cheat Sheet + Solutions

Print this page or save as PDF.

Cheat Sheet

x% of y = (x/100) × y
x is what % of y = (x/y) × 100
Percentage change = (change/base) × 100
If A is R% more than B, then B is R × 100/(100+R)% less than A
If A is R% less than B, then B is R × 100/(100−R)% more than A
Successive increase a% and b% = a+b+(ab/100)%
Successive decrease a% and b% = a+b−(ab/100)%
n years growth = P × (1 + r/100)^n
n years depreciation = P × (1 − r/100)^n
If price increases by r%, consumption should reduce by r×100/(100+r)%
If price decreases by r%, consumption may increase by r×100/(100−r)%

Solutions

Q1. What is 25% of 360?
Correct answer: 90
Explanation: 25% of 360 = 360/4 = 90.
Q2. If 40 is what percent of 160?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: 40/160 × 100 = 25%.
Q3. A value increases from 200 to 250. Increase percentage?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: Increase = 50, base = 200, so 50/200 × 100 = 25%.
Q4. If A is 20% more than B, B is how much less than A?
Correct answer: 16.67%
Explanation: Less% = 20 × 100/(100+20) = 16.67%.
Q5. If A is 25% less than B, then B is how much more than A?
Correct answer: 33.33%
Explanation: More% = 25 × 100/(100−25) = 33.33%.
Q6. Successive increase of 10% and 20% gives net?
Correct answer: 32%
Explanation: 10+20+(10×20)/100 = 32%.
Q7. Successive decrease of 10% and 20% gives net?
Correct answer: 28% decrease
Explanation: 10+20−(10×20)/100 = 28% decrease.
Q8. A population of 5000 grows by 10% in one year. New population?
Correct answer: 5500
Explanation: 5000 × 1.10 = 5500.
Q9. A machine worth 8000 depreciates by 25%. New value?
Correct answer: 6000
Explanation: 8000 × 0.75 = 6000.
Q10. What number is 15% of 200?
Correct answer: 30
Explanation: 15/100 × 200 = 30.
Q11. 50 is what percent of 200?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: 50/200 × 100 = 25%.
Q12. If price rises by 20%, by what percent should consumption fall to keep spend same?
Correct answer: 16.67%
Explanation: 20 × 100/(100+20) = 16.67%.
Q13. If price falls by 20%, by what percent can consumption rise?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: 20 × 100/(100−20) = 25%.
Q14. Find 12.5% of 640.
Correct answer: 80
Explanation: 12.5% = 1/8, so 640/8 = 80.
Q15. A score rises from 80 to 100. Percent increase?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: Increase 20 on base 80 → 25%.
Q16. What is 0.5% of 800?
Correct answer: 4
Explanation: 0.5/100 × 800 = 4.
Q17. A number is reduced by 10% and then increased by 10%. Net change?
Correct answer: 1% loss
Explanation: Successive change = −1%.
Q18. If 30% of x = 45, x = ?
Correct answer: 150
Explanation: x = 45 × 100 / 30 = 150.
Q19. If 18 is 60% of a number, the number is?
Correct answer: 30
Explanation: 18 × 100 / 60 = 30.
Q20. Two successive increases of 5% and 5% equal?
Correct answer: 10.25%
Explanation: 5+5+(5×5)/100 = 10.25%.
Q21. A salary of 40000 is increased by 12.5%. New salary?
Correct answer: 45000
Explanation: 12.5% = 1/8, increase = 5000, new = 45000.
Q22. If 60% of a class are boys and there are 24 boys, class strength?
Correct answer: 40
Explanation: 24 is 60% → total = 24/0.6 = 40.
Q23. What percent is 7 out of 28?
Correct answer: 25%
Explanation: 7/28 × 100 = 25%.
Q24. A product marked 500 gets 20% discount. Selling price?
Correct answer: 400
Explanation: 500 × 0.8 = 400.
Q25. If 80% of a number is 96, number is?
Correct answer: 120
Explanation: 96 × 100 / 80 = 120.
